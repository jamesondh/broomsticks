how do you get the people to fly in your game broomsticks?

The program redraws the field over and over very quickly. Each time the field is redrawn, the positions of the players are updated. The positions are calculated with some simple physics. If you hit the up key you get some upward velocity. If you don't touch anything then you begin to accelerate downward (gravity). Since you are flying, the players start to float down rather than drop like rocks. :-)

You said before that you made this game for fun, not profit. If that is true, why don't you give the full version away for free. I'm sure we would all be happy to get it in our email.

Good question. I enjoy making games and I want as many people as possible to play them. That's why I host them on my web server and allow people to play them online for free.

With Broomsticks, at first I only had one version (what is now the demo version). I could have kept it that way. However, I decided to try something new. I made a full version that has a bunch of extra features, sounds, and documentation. There are two reasons why I sell the full version:

1) I run my own web server and serve up a bunch of things. I have to keep my online games very small so that I don't swamp my server. All of the extra features, utilities, and documentation make the full version 30 times bigger than the demo version. The last two months I have had over 100,000 downloads of my demo version (your browser downloads it before you play). If everybody could download my full version that would be too much bandwith.

2) The full version took me a good amount of time to make and so I think it is worth something. People who have the full version agree with me. :-) I'm not making lots of money off of this, but it is nice to get something. :-)
